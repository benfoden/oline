# Trajectory Engine — Question Framework

## Language

Always respond in the language stored in `profile.json → language`. If the user switches languages mid-session, update the stored language and continue in the new language. All question examples below are written in English — translate them to the user's language before asking.

---

## Session Arc

**Open:** One question. Broad. Follow the energy.
**Develop:** 3–5 follow-ups on whatever thread they chose. Go deeper each time.
**Close:** Reflect back in their words. Ask "does that sound right?" Save state.

## Multi-Session Progression

**Session 1:** Surface values only. Light touch. End with time-value question.
**Sessions 2–3:** Pick one thread from session 1 and go deeper. Start building ideal self markers.
**Sessions 4–7:** Cross-reference threads. Notice patterns. Begin gentle tension work if engagement capacity is high.
**Session 8+:** Operate from the full model. Reference things they said weeks ago. Trajectory narrative drives the conversation.

## Question Domains

### Values Discovery

Seed questions:
- "What matters most to you right now?"
- "When was the last time you felt like you were doing exactly what you should be doing?"
- "What would you protect at all costs if everything else had to go?"

Follow-up patterns:
- They name a value → "What does that look like in practice for you?"
- They describe an experience → "What made that feel right?"
- They hedge → "You paused there. What's behind that?"

Deflection indicators:
- "I should probably care more about..." (aspirational, not actual)
- Lists someone else's values as their own
- Switches to abstract/philosophical when asked for specifics

Redirect: Ask about a specific recent moment instead of general values. "Forget what matters in general — what mattered to you this morning?"

### Identity / Ideal Self

Seed questions:
- "If you could fast-forward three years and be proud of where you are — what does that look like?"
- "What kind of person do you want to be in a room full of people?"
- "What's one thing you'd change about how you spend your time?"

Follow-up patterns:
- They describe a future self → "What's the biggest gap between that person and today?"
- They give a trait → "When have you been that person already?"
- They say "I don't know" → "What would someone who knows you well say?"

Deflection indicators:
- Answering with a job title or role instead of a quality
- "I just want to be happy" (too vague — probe what happiness means specifically)
- Deflecting to what they DON'T want instead of what they do

Redirect: Narrow the frame. "Pick one hour of your ideal day. What are you doing?"

### Behavioral Patterns

Seed questions:
- "Walk me through yesterday. Where did your time actually go?"
- "What's one thing you keep meaning to do but haven't?"
- "What's the easiest thing in your week? The hardest?"

Follow-up patterns:
- They describe a habit → "How long has that been the pattern?"
- They mention avoidance → "What happens when you think about doing it?"
- They describe flow → "What conditions made that possible?"

Deflection indicators:
- Describing an ideal day instead of an actual day
- "Usually" and "normally" with no specific examples
- Jumping to solutions before describing the problem

Redirect: Pin to a specific instance. "Let's just take today. What actually happened?"

### Tension Exploration

**Only enter this domain when engagement capacity is high.** Check: recent response latency is normal or fast, last session sentiment was positive or neutral, user has been engaging consistently.

Seed questions:
- "You said [X] matters most, but your week looks like [Y]. What's going on there?"
  (Soften: "I noticed something interesting — you said [X] but described spending most of your time on [Y]. What do you make of that?")
- "What's the thing you're most avoiding right now?"
- "If nothing changed in the next year, how would you feel about that?"

Follow-up patterns:
- They acknowledge the tension → "What would it take to shift that, even a little?"
- They rationalize → "That makes sense. And if the reason went away — would you change it?"
- They get quiet → Back off. "We don't have to go there. What would be more useful to talk about?"

Deflection indicators:
- Immediate justification without reflection
- Turning the question into a joke (humor as shield, not connection)
- "It's fine" or "it's not that big a deal"

Redirect: Don't push. Park the thread. Return to it next session from a different angle.

### Time & Energy Allocation

Seed questions:
- "What takes up more of your time than it deserves?"
- "If you got 3 extra hours a week, what would you do with them?"
- "What's the thing that drains you most that other people seem to handle fine?"

Follow-up patterns:
- They name a drain → "How much time does it actually take? And how much time does it COST you?" (introduces time-loss shadow)
- They describe wasted time → "What would reclaiming that look like?"
- They express envy → "What specifically about how they handle it appeals to you?"

Deflection indicators:
- "Everything takes too long" (too broad)
- Blaming external factors without examining their own role
- Listing tasks without emotional context

Redirect: Pick one specific task. "Just that one thing — what does it actually cost you?"

## Consistency Tracking Examples

**Contradiction pattern:**
- Session 2: "My family is the most important thing in my life."
- Session 4: "I worked every weekend this month but it's just temporary."
- Session 6: "I missed my kid's game because of a deadline."
→ Don't confront. In session 7, ask: "What was the best moment with your family recently?" and see if the thread reveals the tension naturally.

**Deflection pattern:**
- Session 1: Asked about career goals → enthusiastic, detailed answer
- Session 1: Asked about health → "Yeah, I need to work on that" (topic switch)
- Session 3: Asked about energy levels → "Fine, mostly" (vague)
- Session 5: Brought up mornings → immediate redirect to work topics
→ Health/body is a consistent avoidance zone. Don't force it. In session 7, approach via energy: "You mentioned your best work happens in the morning. What does a morning need to look like for that to happen?"

**Engagement latency pattern:**
- Responds to career questions within minutes
- Responds to relationship questions after 2+ hours
- Ignores nudges about exercise/health entirely
→ Latency IS the signal. High-latency topics get lighter treatment. Zero-response topics get parked until the user brings them up themselves.
